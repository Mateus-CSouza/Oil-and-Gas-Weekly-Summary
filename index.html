💡 Hormuz stalemate deepens: Brent above $110/bbl amid Iran war, Trump attack delay and record SPR draw 🌍
  • US-Iran nuclear talks deadlocked; drone struck UAE nuclear facility; ~4 vessels/day transiting Strait vs. normal ~95/day; war-risk insurance near record highs. Iran negotiating transit toll with European countries — China, Japan, Pakistan already authorized.
  • Trump postponed planned attack on Iran following appeal from Saudi Arabia, Qatar and UAE (requesting 2–3 day delay to close a deal); no Iranian response as of Mon May-19. US issued temporary waiver allowing sale of Russian oil already loaded on vessels (valid to Jun-17) — attempt to stabilize physical market, irritating European allies.
  • Record SPR draw: 9.9mn bbl withdrawn last week → total SPR falls to ~374mn bbl (lowest since Jul/2024); JPM sees supply disruptions exceeding 800mn bbl, Hormuz transit minimal, odds of peace declining.
  • Brent opened week at $110.98/bbl (+1.78%); Brent/WTI +10.1%/+7.7% WoW; volatile session Mon May-19: oil up on Asian open, retreated on Iran waiver, recovered, fell again after Trump attack postponement post.
MFIR take: Positive for O&G cash flows across the portfolio — elevated price floor directly benefits Vista, YPF, Ecopetrol, PRIO, and Brava. SPR depletion reduces US buffer capacity, adding structural upside risk to prices. Risk: escalation tail non-trivial; waiver and attack delay introduce short-term volatility but do not resolve fundamental supply disruption.

  • Targets 5 mb/d production capacity post-conflict; Ceyhan (Turkey) pipeline route selected as primary diversification away from Hormuz-dependent southern export routes.
  • Ceyhan exports targeted to rise from current 200k to 500k b/d — meaningful capacity addition if conflict persists.
MFIR take: Neutral to slightly positive — no direct portfolio impact, but reinforces medium-term supply tightness thesis underpinning elevated prices.

Citgo valuation anchor relevant for creditors. Colombia resilience positive for Ecopetrol, partially offset by political transition risk.

💡 Petrobras (BRZ) | Refining expansion — targeting diesel self-sufficiency by 2031 and export capacity; gasoline price hike imminent 🇧🇷
  • Refining utilization at 103%; plan to add 500k b/d of diesel capacity 2027–2031 (current plan: +330k b/d to 2030, covering 85% of domestic demand); full self-sufficiency in both diesel and gasoline targeted by 2031.

MFIR take: Neutral to positive for PBR credit — refining expansion reduces structural import dependency and improves long-term FCF from downstream. Near-term diesel imports and constrained gasoline pass-through are minor headwinds. Subsidy framework watch item for fiscal impact on PBR economics.
